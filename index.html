The Hackerspaces Tour

aka “The Great Schlep”

Where are we going, and why am I in this handbasket?

I’m writing a book about hackerspaces and hacker culture.

In this project, I am covering the most interesting hackerspaces on both coasts. This includes New York City, Boston, Philadelphia, Washington DC, San Francisco, Oakland, Seattle, and Vancouver.

Along the way, I am taking photos, writing about the positive impact that hackerspaces are having on their communities. I will also be working with various people to teach about privacy, security, and censorship. If you have a suggestion of how to help out, drop me a line.

All photos will be released with a Creative Commons license. When I traveled to Occupy Wall Street, I took more than a thousand photos. With at least eight cities and 30 days on this trip, I expect to shoot more than three thousand photos. Everyone who met me at HacDC can verify that I take way more photos of each space than I really expect to use. In-person, my camera looks hilariously under-powered, but it’s a great camera, takes excellent photos, and is sturdy enough to make the trip!

So… who’s paying for all this?

Me. To conserve funds, I am travelling light and couchsurfing along the way. While I did run a kickstarter, it has not successfully funded (and I don’t expect it to). It’s okay though — if you’ve donated to Kickstarter projects before, you’ve probably got enough t-shirts and tote bags to last you a lifetime.

You can still donate to the project via Paypal or WePay. All donations of at least $30 will be counted as a book pre-order, and donors can remain anonymous with WePay.
